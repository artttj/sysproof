THE REAL FINDING (first time sysproof leads in the whole study):
  Grounding pays ONLY where the requirement is genuinely latent - unknowable without
  reading the code. G3 (FeedError not ValueError) and G4 (dedupe before validate) are
  the discriminators: control fails them 100% (0/10, 0/10) because it never looks;
  sysproof recovers a few (3/10, 1/10) because the skill makes it read newsroom/.
  G2 is a ceiling (model guesses the convention anyway) and G1 is half-guessable, so
  the aggregate lead comes from the hard latent traps - exactly the failure class the
  redesign targets.

  This is the mirror-completion of the whole arc:
   - end-gate (old sysproof): inert, because a competent model already verifies.
   - front-grounding (new sysproof): helps ONLY when the answer is hidden in the code
     and the model would otherwise guess. When the answer is stated, signposted, or
     guessable, grounding is redundant.

POWER: the signal lives in G3/G4 where control is at floor (0/10). With more reps,
even the observed modest sysproof recovery (3/10, 1/10) would likely reach
significance. A scaled rerun (e.g. 30-40 reps on G3/G4, or all four tasks) is the
clean way to confirm or refute. As it stands: promising direction, not yet proven.

Verification (ruled out confound): the 40 sysproof prompts DID carry the full
skill ("GROUND BEFORE YOU BUILD" present); the newsroom repo WAS importable at
the host path. Yet 0/40 sysproof solutions mention `newsroom` or `FeedError` —
every one guessed ValueError/KeyError. The environment was correct; the skill
did not make the model read the repo.

Statistics: P(0 of 40 | true rate 30%) = 6.4e-7. The run-8 result of 3/10 was
small-sample noise. True G3 reuse/correctness rate under grounding is <~5%,
indistinguishable from control's structural 0%.

REVISED CONCLUSION: run 8's aggregate lead (sysproof 24/40 vs control 18/40)
was driven by underpowered per-task noise. G3 — the cleanest latent-only
discriminator — shows ZERO effect at power. A generic "ground before you build"
instruction does NOT reliably trigger codebase research on a task the model
thinks it already knows (raising an error). The model's prior overrides the skill.

The redesign, like the original end-gate, appears INERT under proper power. The
honest verdict for the whole study: neither half of sysproof (end-gate OR
front-grounding) produces a measurable benefit on a competent model. Pending G4
at power as final confirmation, but G3's collapse predicts the same.
